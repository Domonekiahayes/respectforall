As shown in the picture above, addiction to drugs can occur in a numerous amount of ways.

Facts

  • Addiction is a chronic disease that creates dramatic changes in the brain

  • Addiction is a result of changes in the brain

  • Not everyone who uses substances is addicted

  • People who are addicted to drugs come in every age, color, shape, gender, background, and economic status

  • In 2015, prescription pain killers were the most abused drug after Marijuana. Abusing opioid medication can easily lead to dependence and ultimately addiction.

  • Many people suffer from addiction and continue working

  • Any drug when taken in excess can cause damage to the brain and the body

  • Family and loved ones are often the people who are in the best position to offer support to the addicted individual

  • While rehab is not a lifetime cure, it is a highly effective form of treatment.

  • Recovery from a drug addiction is a lifelong process. Relapse should not be viewed as a failure.

Myths

  • Addicts Can End Use Whenever They Wish

  • Addiction Is a Moral Failure

  • All People that Use Drugs Are Addicted

  • Addicts Are Easy to Identify

  • You Cannot Be Addicted to a Prescribed Medication

  • If You Can Go to Work, You Are Not Really Addicted

  • Only “Hard” Drugs are Dangerous

  • There is Nothing Friends or Family Can Do to Help

  • Rehab Doesn’t Really Work

  • A Relapse Equals Failure
